Timbre

Website

Goal

Help musicians looking to form a band or play music with other musicians meet musicians in their area.

About

Users can play sample sounds from nearby users. Users are notified if they both "like" each other's music.

Requirements

I developed Timbre with a group of four other students for my final Web Programming project at Tufts University. Before creating the wireframes, we worked together to iron out the details and use cases for Timbre. We wanted to keep the initial website fairly simple. However, we did want users to be able to specify their search radius and filter their results by type of instrument. With these requirements in mind, I put together some intial wireframes for Timbre.

The Final Website

The final project uses both server-side and client-side data persistance (MongoDB and Local Storage). It also uses the Google Maps, SoundCloud, Facebook, and Mozilla Notifications APIs. Currently, users can only log in with Facebook. In the future, I want to let users create their own accounts on Timbre and upload their own music, rather than just link to SoundCloud. I would also like to implement a messaging feature to make it easier for users to connect.
