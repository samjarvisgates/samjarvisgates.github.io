Trend Mobile App

My New App Idea

Trend is a Mobile App designed to target twenty-something year-old consumers. These consumers tend to be on Instagram and often use Pinterest to find cool clothing as well as Wanelo, a popular social shopping site.

Trend works in a similar way to Instagram and looks more like a social networking app than a shopping site. After talking to some Wanelo shoppers, I decided to enhance the social networking components of Trend to foster a stronger community and keep users coming back.

Initial Concepts

The initial version is location-based, and users can filter between recent and popular posts, much like Yik Yak. Trend includes a feature to “explore” fashion styles in different locations, at certain events, or in upcoming movies.

 
I began sketching out the main newsfeed and navigation bar for the app. From these sketches, I used Illustrator to turn these sketches into wireframes. I uploaded these wireframes to InVision and created a clickable prototype. I sent this clickable prototype to four of my friends and asked them a number of questions about how they might use the app and if the navigation was easy to understand.

The Prototype

Incorporating the feedback from the wireframe, I put together a high fidelity prototype and sent it out to more friends. I continue to iterate on this prototype. Before final development of this app, I plan to test it with a larger audience and understand more precisely how people would use it.
