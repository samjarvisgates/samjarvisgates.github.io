Splender

From June through August 2015, I completed an internship at Cartera Commerce. Cartera Commerce provides shopping rewards programs for retail companies and airlines. As an intern, I worked on a B2C product called Splender.

How It Works

The first version of Splender worked as follows:

  1. A customer would search Splender for a store they wanted to shop at, such as Nordstrom
  2. The customer would then click out of Splender and go to the Nordstrom site
  3. When the customer bought something on Nordstrom, Nordstrom recognized that the customer came from Splender
  4. In return for sending them customers, Nordstrom gives a percentage of the purchase price back to Splender
  5. Splender then gives half of this percentage back to the customer

First Steps

My first task with Splender was to work with the product manager to identify areas to improve the shopping experience of Splender. Weekly, we consulted with beta testers from our target audience and learned that people wanted to search for products on Splender, not just stores.

 
Based on these results, I started designing a product search feature for Splender. I researched how other ecommerce sites implemented product search. I read usability studies published by Baymard and sketched out some rough wireframes.

The Prototypes

After showing sketches to the beta testers, I created my first high-fidelity clickable prototype for product searches on Splender. During weekly sessions and iterations with the beta-testers, the initial product-search prototype was improved, with help from the design team. I also created and began testing responsive, mobile, versions of the Splender webpage.

The End

After iterating for weeks, the final prototype was presented to the engineering, legal, and marketing teams. The final product search designs were selected and are currently being developed.

The App

During the process of improving the Splender website, the product manager and I also decided to create some designs for a Splender app. Unlike the website, the app would focus more on favoriting and creating a more personalized experience for the Splender user. We began testing different ideas and prototypes.
